5 Spa Hotels in Kent for a Relaxing Weekend

Having lived in Kent my whole life, I can personally attest to its beauty and tranquillity, despite its proximity to London.

If you’re after a rejuvenating spa break, then the Kent countryside offers a handful of lovely hotels and spas for a dreamy getaway.

In this article, I share my pick of the best spa hotels in Kent to help you de-stress and return to your best self.

Featured hotels:

  • Hempstead House Hotel & Spa
  • Yarrow Hotel
  • The Spa Hotel Tunbridge Wells
  • Brandshatch Place Hotel & Spa
  • Rowhill Grange Hotel & Utopia Spa

5 Best Spa Hotels in Kent If you’re looking to recharge and heal while in the beautiful Kentish countryside, look no further than the following hotels and spas:
Hempstead House Hotel & Spa An hour away from London, Hempstead House offers the loveliest atmosphere in the Kent countryside. This hotel has an in-house restaurant, luxury rooms, and high-tech spa experiences, making it the perfect destination to unplug and relax. The spa feels like a mini oasis where you can easily lose track of time. The attentive staff are also great at guiding you through the facilities and suggesting treatments tailored to your needs. You can pick between specialist massage treatments, detoxing body wraps, and even facials. For the mums-to-be, Hempstead’s Aquamanda spa also offers the most special treatments to take the weight off of your feet.
Yarrow Hotel The Yarrow Hotel’s Jacobethan architecture might surprise you since it’s a spa, but it beautifully blends historical charm with modern luxury. After a luxurious spa experience, complete with a facial and tasty pastries, you can head out to the beach, which is only a walking distance from the hotel. Even if you’re not staying in the Yarrow Hotel, I’d make the trip just to enjoy a meal at the hotel’s fine dining establishment.
The Spa Hotel Tunbridge Wells If you want to enjoy the gorgeous nature and step away from the city, the Spa Hotel at Tunbridge Wells is set on 14 acres of green ground and offers some of the best wellness services. There’s an array of treatments, fitting all preferences. I’m particularly keen on the Afternoon Tea Experience, where you can choose from a 25-minute express treatment paired with soothing herbal tea. If you’re in dire need of winding down, I’d definitely recommend the Indulgence Ritual Experience.
Brandshatch Place Hotel & Spa The luxury Brandshatch Place Hotel and Spa has it all, from luxurious spa and wellness treatments to mesmerising gardens and scenery. Whether you’re travelling for business or leisure, this hotel will have the service for you. The treatments range from quick 20-minute pedicures to full-body massages. There’s also a gym with spectacular classes, inspiring anyone to kickstart their wellness journey. The only downside I found at Brandshatch is the difficult choice between the spa, jacuzzi, hot tub, and sauna!
Rowhill Grange Hotel & Utopia Spa As a luxury lover, the Rowhill Grange Hotel and Utopia Spa is a favourite. The top-notch therapists provide a pampering service that can melt away years worth of stress. The highlight of Rowhill Grange, however, is the Roman bath theme, complemented by impeccable facilities. This makes the unique country retreat perfect for celebrations, romantic getaways, or a relaxing trip for the whole family.
To Conclude A visit to one of Kent's luxurious spa hotels is sure to leave you refreshed and rejuvenated. You’ll find plenty of exceptional establishments tucked away in the countryside. There, you’ll be able to connect with nature outdoors, then retreat to the spa and pamper yourself at the hotel in the evening.

Get Paid to Move to France

Moving to France seems straight out of a movie, but you can easily make this dream come true.

In fact, you can get paid to relocate, whether you're looking for an exciting job opportunity or want to buy a house in the French countryside.

France offers exceptional social benefits, an enviable work-life balance, and exciting opportunities for expats.

This article explores relocation incentives, cities worth considering, careers that can support your move, and what life in France is really like.

Can You Get Paid to Move to France?

Many jobs in France offer a competitive relocation package. The country rarely pays new residents, but it's still exceptionally welcoming to immigrants and expats.

Only one area in France can pay you 1,000 euros per year for five years. You need to be willing to buy a home in Aisne and relocate permanently.

While you might not get paid to move to France, you'll quickly get settled if you're an EU citizen. If you're a non-EU citizen, you can still move to the country if you have a work permit.

After living there for a while, you'll be able to apply for a residence permit, or a long-stay visa, and social security.

Are There Tax Benefits to Moving to France?

While the French social security system is generous, it comes with high tax rates. Unfortunately, there aren't any substantial tax benefits to moving to France.

There are three types of taxes in France:

  • Income tax, ranging from 0 to 45%
  • Social security contribution
  • Tax on goods and services (VAT)

Still, taxes are pretty hassle-free in France. That's because the country utilizes the PAYE (Pay As You Earn) system. Instead of calculating and filing your taxes annually, the taxes get deducted from your monthly salary.

Places in France That Pay You to Move

Here are some French cities that might pay you to relocate:

Aisne

The historic Aisne department lies in the Haute-de-France region. The area, formerly known as Picardie, has a relatively low population. Accordingly, new residents can get paid a hefty sum by relocating to the North of France.

How Much You Can Make

Aisne is willing to pay up to 5,000 euros for new residents of one of its 40 municipalities. Obtaining this relocation grant isn't easy.

You need to purchase a home in this sparsely populated region. You must also be a first-time property owner in France.

It's best to consult a real estate agent, as there are eligibility requirements for your new home in Haute-de-France, including:

  • The home must have been on sale for at least three years.
  • Your new Aisne home must be your primary residence for at least five years.
  • If the prospective owner moves out before the five years, they must pay €1,000 for every year the house remains unoccupied.

What Makes This Option Great

You might think the relocation fees aren't a lot, considering you need to buy a property to even be eligible. However, you'll get to live in the beautiful French countryside, own a beautiful home, and get a relocation incentive.

That's not all. To encourage families to settle in the area, Aisne provides everything you might need, including fiber optic internet.

Paris

When you think of France, the first city that comes to mind is Paris. The capital is home to over one million expats. There are also various job opportunities for non-French speakers. So, you'll find your place there.

How Much You Can Make

Paris might be one of the most expensive cities, considering it's a tourist hub. Still, salaries and job opportunities are much higher than in other European cities.

The average annual salary in Paris is 54,100 euros. You'll find open positions in high-paying industries such as tech, finance, and insurance.

As if that wasn't enough, Paris is a great city for creative expats, as you'll find open positions in luxury goods, fashion, and cosmetics.

What Makes This Option Great

Paris is one of the best cities to live in, especially if you're a fan of arts and culture. The City of Love is the global center for history, fashion, and gastronomy.

The nightlife in Paris is incredibly lively. That's why the French capital makes for a perfect home for everyone, regardless of their interests.

Lyon

Lyon is one of the best locations for expats. The attractive French city is a popular spot for retirees due to its accessible living, vibrant activities, and stunning nature.

How Much You Can Make

Lyon doesn't pay you to move, but the living costs are more affordable than in other French cities, and the salaries are high. The average annual salary in Lyon is 50,600 euros.

Lyon is an economically strong city. It's a hub for numerous industries such as banking, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, and biotechnology. Naturally, the jobs there are well-paying.

Though Lyon isn't the cheapest city in France, it's 20% cheaper than Paris, while still being as culturally and economically significant as the French capital.

What Makes This Option Great

Despite Lyon being a small city, it's full of life, culture, and delicious food, making it one of the best French cities for expats. It's home to many underrated historical landmarks, as well as an energetic nightlife.

Simply put, Lyon is comparable to Paris, only it's less densely populated and has a relatively cheaper price tag.

Jobs in France That Pay You to Move

If you're a qualified candidate, most job positions will offer you a relocation incentive.

Still, if you're unsure where to start when it comes to moving to France, here are some of the best jobs that pay you to move:

Gîte Manager

How Much You Can Make

Depending on the type and location of your gîte, you can make a variable income.

What Makes This Option Great

Managing a vacation home gives you total flexibility. You're basically managing yourself.

Au Pair

How Much You Can Make

The French guidelines recommend an au pair salary of about 320 euros a month.

What Makes This Option Great

You don't need any experience to work as an au pair. Families are mostly interested in exposing their children to new cultures.

Finance

How Much You Can Make

The average annual salary for finance occupations in France is about 55,000 euros.

What Makes This Option Great

There is no shortage of finance occupations in Paris or Lyon, and many employers offer relocation packages.

Remote Work

How Much You Can Make

If you're a remote worker, your salary will generally remain the same after moving to France.

What Makes This Option Great

France offers excellent infrastructure for digital nomads, including high-speed internet, cafés, and coworking spaces.

Why You Should Consider Moving to France

Rich History

Moving to France allows you to immerse yourself in centuries of art, architecture, and culture.

Beautiful Location

From the French Riviera to the countryside villages, France offers some of the most beautiful scenery in Europe.

Exceptional Healthcare

France has one of the best healthcare systems in the world and offers affordable healthcare to eligible residents.

Work-Life Balance

France is known for its 35-hour work week, generous paid leave, and strong employee protections.

Unique Culture

Whether you love fashion, art, food, or nightlife, France offers a lifestyle that appeals to a wide range of interests.

Is It Cheap to Live in France?

Living in France can be expensive, especially in major cities such as Paris and Lyon. However, healthcare, transportation, and social benefits can offset some of these costs.

What Is a Livable Salary in France?

An annual salary of approximately 38,000 euros is generally considered enough for a single person to live comfortably in France.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can You Move to France Without a Job?

You can move to France without a job if you're an EU, EEA, or Swiss citizen. Non-EU citizens generally need a visa and proof of employment or financial support.

How Can I Move to France Permanently?

After living and working in France legally for several years, you may be eligible for permanent residency or French citizenship.

Wrapping Up

France is an exceptional country for expats. Though many people want to move there for the art, history, and cuisine, the country also offers excellent healthcare, social security benefits, and a strong work-life balance.

While only a few regions provide direct relocation incentives, many employers offer relocation packages, making France an attractive destination for professionals, creatives, and remote workers alike.
